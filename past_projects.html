Stand, Walk, Drive

Stand, Walk, Drive was an mobile application I had thought about developing. At the time I lacked android developing experience, but hackUCI gave me the opportunity to learn from others so I pitched the idea to three other students and we got together and prototyped the application. The purpose of the application was to use the smartphone's existing accelerometer to determine whether a person was lucid enough to drive. We managed to finish a working but rudimentary application.

The prototype which was built in Eclipse was able to objectively determine whether a person was lucid enough to drive by having them walk in a straight line for a few seconds. We gave the application arbitrary values to determine how much sway a person could have in their walking. If the opportunity should present itself, I would like to continue the application with user research and testing to give the application more accurate values in the hopes that it prevents people from attempting to drive while under the influence of alcohol and other intoxicants.

Embedded Systems Final Project

Since we had creative freedom for our final project, I decided to draw inspiration from action and spy movies from my childhood and created a lazer trip alarm. I say lazer trip alarm but I took it a different direction and made it a general light sensative trip alarm that would sample the light levels whether it be from a lazer of regular room lighting. Any deviation from light levels such as increase or decrease light levels would trigger the alarm.

The project consists of the program written in C in atmel studios. And the hardware components were embedded onto a breadboard. The LCD was to display the settings that took user input from the numpad. Its secondary function was to display when the alarm was tripped.
This project made use of:

  • A numpad for input
  • A LCD for output
  • A speaker for output
  • A crystal for time keeping
  • A photoresistor for input
  • Other parts include, programmer, regulator, resistor, 9v battery, and LED as indicator
  • Lastly my induction speaker to amplify the regular speaker
